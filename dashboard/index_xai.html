Key Research Findings

Critical insights from the 7-model comparative analysis on EUR/USD forecasting performance.

1

Hybrid Supremacy with Optimized GARCH

The complete ARIMA-GARCH(2,1)-LSTM hybrid achieves 56.10% directional accuracy after GARCH order optimization, demonstrating that empirically-derived model specifications combined with multi-stage architectures significantly outperform single-method approaches. The +1.02% gain validates ACF/PACF-based model selection.

2

Volatility Expertise

GARCH-based models excel at uncertainty quantification, with ARIMA-GARCH achieving 95.46% coverage. This makes them essential for risk management applications despite lower directional accuracy.

3

Deep Learning Limitations

Standalone LSTM networks underperform (33.87% accuracy, 0.005124 RMSE), revealing that pure deep learning struggles with financial time series without econometric grounding or explicit volatility modeling.

4

Sequential Integration

The three-stage pipeline (ARIMA ‚Üí GARCH ‚Üí LSTM) systematically addresses trend, volatility, and non-linear residuals. Each stage contributes specialized expertise to the final ensemble.

5

Overfitting Patterns

All models show validation-test performance gaps. LSTM-based architectures exhibit more severe overfitting, requiring careful regularization (dropout 0.2, early stopping) to maintain generalization.

6

Production Recommendations

Deploy ARIMA-GARCH-LSTM for point forecasts (best accuracy), but maintain ARIMA-GARCH for interval forecasts (superior coverage). Multi-model ensembles provide robust real-world performance.
